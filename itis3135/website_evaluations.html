Website Evaluations

National Geographic

This website, belonging to National Geographic, contains publicly available information and resources to the National Geographic product lineup: articles, newsletters, shows, podcasts, etc. This is a lot of information to keep organized in one place, so let's take a look at how the CRAP principles apply to this website.

  • C: Contrast

    National Geographic has some appealing contrast in their site, but also some not-so-appealing contrast. Starting off with a positive, the text size if very well done. I think the site header could be a little bigger, but aside from that the text size flows with the content of the page. Scrolling down the page, each segment also has clear contrast with the segments bordering above and below. Now for the negatives. My main gripe with this page is the use of black and white. Although they do technically contrast, the black in the background makes everything pop a little too much.

  • R: Repetition

    They've done a great job in this category. Although I find the infinite scroller site trope (I think originally "coined" by Apple?) a little frustrating to navigate through time-wise, I can't deny that the sections are well designed and, once reached, easily navigable.

  • A: Alignment

    There's not too much to say on this topic, as there isn't too much wrong to complain about. The sub-headers are all centered, along with their captions, and the content beneath are sectioned off in a clean and organized alignment.

  • P: Proximity

    Similar to alignment, there's not much to complain about here. Each section takes up nearly the entire webpage, and all the content is close in the center, with ample use of empty space in the margins on either side. Each header is also close enough to its content to easily imply connection.
